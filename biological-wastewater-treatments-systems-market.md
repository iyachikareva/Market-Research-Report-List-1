<p><p>Biological wastewater treatment systems utilize microorganisms to break down organic pollutants. Aerobic systems operate in the presence of oxygen, promoting aerobic bacteria to decompose waste, often resulting in higher treatment efficiency and faster processes. Anaerobic systems function without oxygen, utilizing anaerobic bacteria to convert organic matter into biogas and sludge, ideal for high-strength wastewater. Both systems cater to different treatment needs, influencing the selection of technology based on factors such as waste composition, regulatory requirements, and energy considerations.</p></p>
