Amorphous silicon solar cells, characterized by their thin-film design, offer flexibility and efficient performance in low light conditions, making them suitable for indoor environments. Photochemical solar cells utilize chemical reactions triggered by light to convert energy, providing an alternative method for capturing solar power indoors. Both types cater to the growing demand for renewable energy solutions in urban and indoor settings.</p></p>
